ABOUT US

The year is 1994 . It is the United Nation’s International Year of the Family, Nelson Mandela is elected President of South Africa, and FIFA establishes the Golden Goal rule. Meanwhile in Ghana, some people meet to form the foundation of an association to support foreign spouses and their Ghanaian families.

Though the countries they were born in might differ, what they have in common is that they are married to Ghanaians and have decided to make Ghana their home.

Their vision is that foreign spouses needs a support system, an association in which the diversity of its members can be celebrated and the members’ commitment to Ghana can be promoted. The International Spouses’ Association of Ghana is born.

Ghana is our home!




OUR MISSION

ISAG’s mission is to provide a support system, social network and advocacy platform for foreign spouses of Ghanaians so that they can maintain their commitment to Ghana. ISAG enhances its members’ ability to sustain their partnership with their spouses, fulfill their custodianship of their children, and participate fully in the social, cultural and economic life in Ghana.


OUR COMMITMENT

Our Commitment As foreign spouses of Ghanaians, we advocate and promote the full legal rights of our members to live and work as Ghanaian spouses do. We are members of Ghanaian families and therefore uphold the importance of the family in the full development of the individual and the nation.

As parents of Ghanaian children, we assist our members in their special responsibility of nurturing citizens of dual heritage. We offer the additional support that a member’s family would normally provide as we are individuals away from their native homes.

As men and women of different cultures and traditions, we foster mutual respect, understanding and friendship between our members’ own and our adopted Ghanaian culture.

As responsible members of the community, we align ourselves with local and international groups who aspire to attain greater human dignity and gender equity and work for its expression in everyday life.





MILESTONES & ACHIEVEMENTS

Our two major advocacy success happened in 2000 and 2013. In 2000, we achieved the adaptation by the government of the Indefinite Residence Permit for foreign spouses.

In 2013, we achieved the exemption from the foreign investment criteria requirements in the GIPC legislation which makes it much more feasible for Ghanaian spouses to establish a business in Ghana.

We also realized a gender neutral construction of the immigration law that made foreign male spouses’ entitlements equal to that of the foreign female spouse. This change in the Constitution gives foreign male spouse the right to citizenship, which was previously limited to foreign female spouses.

Finally, we were able to convince the legislators that the definition of spouse should include foreigners who had been married to Ghanaians. Hence the inclusion of “is or has been” in certain legislation, e.g. a foreign spouse who is or has been married to a Ghanaian.
