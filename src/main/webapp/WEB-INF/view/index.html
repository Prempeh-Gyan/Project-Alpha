ISAG NEWS | ISAG meets with the First Lady - Her Excellency Madame Akufo-Addo


Message from the ISAG President
Dear ISAG members and potential members,

As we all know, ISAG was founded over twenty years ago as a self-help association to assist foreign spouses to integrate fully into Ghanaian society and to lead productive lives as members of their Ghanaian families, their communities, and the nation.
It was clear then, as it is now, that foreign spouses need a support system and an association that can advocate for their interests.
As many of you know, ISAG played a key role in changing the law to recognize foreign spouses as a unique group to be eligible for the Indefinite Residence Permit, and later we advocated and achieved the foreign spouses’ exemption from the GIPC capital investment requirements for foreigners who are setting up a business.
These were major accomplishments for ISAG on behalf of foreign spouses. However, there is still quite a lot of work to do to in the area of advocacy. For example, ISAG is now committed to working with Ghana’s leaders to ensure that foreign spouses of Ghanaians and their children continue to be recognized as a unique group under the law as the country develops a new National Identify Card and Non-Citizen Card.

Two weeks ago, as part of our advocacy efforts, a small delegation of ISAG members paid a courtesy call to Ghana’s First Lady, Her Excellency Madame Akufo-Addo. The goal of our visit was to introduce ISAG to the First Lady, share with her our mission and history, and ask for her help and guidance on key advocacy issues. We had a very positive reception and are optimistic that she will follow through on her verbal commitment to assist ISAG in meeting with relevant leaders and institutions to hear our position.

We see this as a very important step; however, we also recognize that our ability to maintain our core mandate requires a solid and engaged membership. Our strength is in our numbers, and our numbers is our membership! I am writing to you today, to share this exciting news and to let everyone know that we are actively pursuing an audience with the heads of key institutions on the Non-Citizen Card issue.

I am also writing to let you know that while the Executive Council has been very busy, and though we’ve had good attendance at meetings and events, our active membership numbers are low. Out of 244 potential members, only 26 people have paid their 2017 membership dues. To be able to cover our costs, to create a vibrant social space for foreign spouses and families, to strengthen our business network of foreign spouses, and to advocate on important issues we need to receive membership dues.
Send an email to isag1994ghana@gmail.com about how to make payment using different methods, and we hope that you will follow up and register/renew your membership.

Finally, I am writing to let members know that in late December, Alan Beales and his son were involved in a bad motorcycle accident. While the son is fine, Alan’s broken arm is taking a bit longer to heal. Please join me in wishing him well and expressing our hope that we will see him soon.

Anneke will be following up shortly with an email about upcoming events, including an Easter get-together and a Car Boot Sale at Baatsona at the end of the month. Have a great start to the week!

Best, Rebecca ISAG President
